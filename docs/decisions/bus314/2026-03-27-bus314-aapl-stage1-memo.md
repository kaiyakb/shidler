## Executive Summary (150 words max)

This memo proposes a comprehensive accounting ratio analysis of **Apple Inc. (AAPL)**, the world's largest publicly traded technology company by market capitalization. Apple designs, manufactures, and markets consumer electronics, software, and digital services across global markets. Using data from Apple's most recent 10-K filing (FY 2025, ending September 2025), this analysis will compute 25+ ratios across six categories — performance, profitability, efficiency, leverage, liquidity, and Du Pont decomposition — to assess Apple's financial health beyond what raw financial statements reveal. The goal is to surface actionable insights on capital efficiency, margin sustainability, and balance sheet strength that can inform strategic capital allocation and operational decisions.

## Company Background & Objectives

Apple Inc. (NASDAQ: AAPL) operates in the **consumer technology** industry, generating revenue through hardware products (iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple Watch) and a rapidly growing Services segment (App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, Apple TV+). For FY 2025, Apple reported over $390 billion in net sales, making it one of the most financially significant companies to analyze.

A comprehensive ratio analysis is valuable because raw financial statements show *what happened* but not *how efficiently or sustainably* it happened. Ratios normalize these figures, enabling comparison across time periods, competitors, and industries. For Apple specifically, this analysis will help evaluate whether its premium pricing power translates into superior returns on capital, how effectively it manages its massive asset base, and whether its aggressive share buyback program has altered its leverage profile.

## Approach

This analysis will draw from Apple's **Consolidated Balance Sheet, Income Statement, and Cash Flow Statement** as reported in the FY 2025 10-K filing. Ratios will be organized into six categories:

1. **Performance** — Measures overall returns to shareholders and capital providers (e.g., EPS, ROE, ROA).
2. **Profitability** — Evaluates margin quality at each level of the income statement (e.g., gross margin, net margin, EBIT margin).
3. **Efficiency** — Assesses how productively Apple uses its assets and manages working capital (e.g., asset turnover, inventory turnover, receivables turnover).
4. **Leverage** — Examines the company's debt structure and financial risk (e.g., debt-to-equity, interest coverage, debt ratio).
5. **Liquidity** — Tests Apple's ability to meet short-term obligations (e.g., current ratio, quick ratio, cash ratio).
6. **Du Pont Decomposition** — Breaks ROE into its component drivers (profit margin, asset turnover, equity multiplier) to pinpoint what is driving returns.

All ratios will be computed using both current-year and prior-year data to identify trends. Market data (share price, shares outstanding) will supplement the financial statements where needed.

## Potential Improvements

The following enhancements could strengthen the analysis in subsequent stages:

1. **Peer Benchmarking** — Compare Apple's ratios against direct competitors (Microsoft, Alphabet, Samsung) and industry medians. Relative positioning reveals whether Apple's metrics reflect company-specific advantages or sector-wide trends.

2. **Adjusted Equity Metrics** — Apple's aggressive share buyback program has driven total equity negative or near-zero in recent years, inflating ROE and distorting leverage ratios. Computing ratios on an adjusted equity basis (adding back cumulative buybacks) would provide a clearer picture of underlying capital efficiency.

3. **Segment-Level Profitability** — Apple's Services segment operates at roughly 70%+ gross margins versus ~35% for Products. A blended ratio analysis masks this divergence. Breaking out segment-level margins and contribution metrics would surface the real growth and profitability drivers.

4. **Multi-Year Trend Analysis (3–5 Years)** — Expanding from a 2-year comparison to a 3–5 year trend window would reveal cyclical patterns, margin trajectory, and whether current performance is an outlier or a sustained trend.

5. **Cash Flow Ratios** — The current six categories omit cash-flow-based metrics. Adding free cash flow yield, operating cash flow ratio, and cash flow to debt would capture Apple's cash generation strength — arguably its most distinctive financial characteristic.

6. **Scenario Sensitivity** — Stress-testing key ratios (e.g., ROE, interest coverage, current ratio) under assumptions like a 5% margin compression or a 10% revenue decline would demonstrate analytical depth and prepare for real-world volatility.

7. **Qualitative Context Overlay** — Tying ratio movements to known business events (e.g., iPhone launch cycles, App Store regulatory rulings, supply chain disruptions) would move the analysis from descriptive to explanatory.
