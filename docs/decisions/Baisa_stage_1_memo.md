---

## Company Background & Objectives

A ratio analysis will be beneficial for the company because it converts 
financial statement data into indicators of performance and risk. Although 
raw financial statements show us large amounts of information, it does not 
clearly show whether the company is generating strong returns, using its 
assets efficiently, relying too heavily on debt, or maintaining liquidity 
well. This analysis allows management to look at their trends over time, 
compare the company to their competitors, and identify their strengths and 
weaknesses that might require action.

---

## Approach

The analysis will examine six ratio categories: performance, profitability, 
efficiency, leverage, liquidity, and DuPont. The performance ratio evaluates 
whether Microsoft is creating value for shareholders through measures like 
EVA and MVA. The profitability ratio shows how effectively the company 
converts their sales, assets, and equity into earnings. Efficiency relation 
measures how well the company uses its assets and manages working capital. 
Leverage ratios assess the company's use of debt and its financial risks. 
Liquidity ratios will determine whether the company can meet its short-term 
obligations. Lastly, the DuPont analysis breaks ROE into profit margin, asset 
turnover, and leverage to determine its driving returns.

For this project, the financial data will come from Microsoft's annual report 
filed with the U.S. SEC through the EDGAR system, and I will also use 
information from Yahoo Finance.
